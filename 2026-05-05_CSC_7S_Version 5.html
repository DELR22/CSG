Section C · How Might We Questions

Frame the challenge as an opportunity

A 'How Might We' question reframes a user challenge as a design opportunity. Write up to two. Each HMW question will generate a separate ideation track in the next section.

Complete the Synthesis section to see your reference here.

A good HMW question is broad enough to allow multiple solutions, but specific enough to point toward a real design direction. The 'without' clause acknowledges what makes this hard and keeps your question from becoming a solution.

Synthesis · Write Your Value Proposition

You've researched your users, identified their shared challenges, framed them as opportunities, generated and selected your best ideas, and specified how they work together. Now distil everything into one sentence — the single promise this design makes.

Try: "A space that [your design move] — so that [your primary user] can [their core aspiration]."

"A workplace where analog wisdom, mindful presence, and post-digital fluency share the same floor — and make each other better."

"A home that bends the boundary between inside and outside, so a family of introverts can breathe."

"A retreat that holds the tension between solitude and connection — giving each person exactly as much of both as they need."
